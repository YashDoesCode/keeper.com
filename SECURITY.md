# Security Policy

At Keeper Sentinel, we are committed to ensuring the security and privacy of our users. We value the contributions of security researchers and the community in helping us maintain a secure environment.

## Supported Versions

As a web-based service, only the most current version of Keeper Sentinel deployed on our official website is supported. Vulnerabilities should always be reported against the live site.

| Version | Supported          |
|---------|--------------------|
| Current | :white_check_mark: |
| Older   | :x:                |

## Reporting a Security Vulnerability

If you believe you have discovered a security vulnerability in our project, we encourage you to report it to us privately. Please **do not** report security vulnerabilities through public GitHub issues.

Instead, please email your findings to **security@keepersentinel.com**.

### What to Include in Your Report

To help us triage and resolve the issue as quickly as possible, please include the following information in your report:

-   **A clear description** of the vulnerability, including its potential impact.
-   **The specific URL or component** where the vulnerability was found.
-   **Detailed steps to reproduce the vulnerability**, including any proof-of-concept code, screenshots, or videos.
-   **Your contact information** for any follow-up questions.

## Our Commitment

When you report a vulnerability, you can expect the following from us:

1.  We will acknowledge receipt of your report within **48 hours**.
2.  We will provide an initial assessment of the vulnerability's severity and validity within **5 business days**.
3.  We will maintain an open line of communication and keep you updated on our progress toward a fix.
4.  We will notify you when the vulnerability has been resolved.

## Scope

This policy applies to the Keeper Sentinel website and its related client-side source code. While we appreciate any reports, the following are considered out-of-scope:

-   Denial of Service (DoS) attacks.
-   Social engineering or phishing attacks.
-   Physical attacks on our infrastructure.
-   Vulnerabilities in third-party services or applications we use.
-   Reports from automated scanners with no manual analysis.

## Safe Harbor

We will not pursue legal action against you if you act in good faith and adhere to the guidelines in this policy. This includes:

-   Not accessing, modifying, or exfiltrating data that does not belong to you.
-   Not disrupting our services or harming our users.
-   Providing us with a reasonable amount of time to resolve the issue before any public disclosure.

We appreciate your efforts in helping us keep our community safe. Thank you for your contribution to the security of Keeper Sentinel.
